Q10 3F / N / L How do you prioritise?

Target: 125 sec. 1,500 UK startups opener = scale anchor. Three filters / No / Lighthouse.

"At Google I was aligned to about 1,500 UK startups when I joined. That number teaches you portfolio discipline — you can engage seriously with maybe 30 or 40 customers in any quarter, so where you spend your time matters as much as how you execute each deal.

I run three filters in parallel.

Signal filter: I track market triggers. Funding rounds in the last 90 days, board changes, public AI commitments, hired AI leadership. That tells me who's allocating budget to AI, not just curious about it.

Qualification filter: I use BANT mechanics, but the real question I'm answering is 'does this customer have a thesis on AI?' If they can clearly answer 'where do you think AI changes your business in 18 months?' — that's a Q1 priority. If they're still at 'we want to explore AI,' they're worth a discovery call but not a quarter of attention.

Size filter: T-shirt sizing on architecture and volume in the first conversation. Video versus text workloads — video is materially higher revenue. 24/7 production versus batch. Customer-facing versus internal. You can size a deal roughly in twenty minutes of technical discovery, and that tells you whether to invest a week or a quarter.

What I'm most disciplined about is what I say no to: 'Wanting to use AI' isn't enough. They need a use case, a budget, an executive champion, and a credible path to production. Missing any of those four — I'd rather invest the cycle in a customer who has all four.

The leverage point — lighthouse weighting: I weight lighthouse-capable accounts heavily. A customer who could become a public reference unlocks five or ten future deals. A transactional deal only unlocks itself.

That discipline matters even more at Anthropic — smaller team, more precious engineering and applied AI hours. I'd protect those resources by pre-qualifying hard before I bring a customer into a deeper engagement."

First try: "I'm on a 50/50 plan at Google with my package weighted toward equity given the front-loaded RSU schedule. Happy to share specifics at offer stage if useful."
If he insists: "I'd really rather have the role conversation first — that's what tells me what's actually relevant to compare. Comp specifics make more sense once we both know where this would land."
If he keeps pushing: "I'm calibrating against your published range — that's what's relevant. Can we work from your band rather than my current number?"

Why this matters: if you share £134k, his internal note becomes "Amy will be happy with anything above current." If you withhold, his internal note becomes "Amy is calibrating against top of band — flagging for hiring manager." That single difference = £20–30k in your base.

"Realistically, between the rest of the interview process, my notice period, and summer holidays, late summer is most likely."

When you hear a number you don't love, default response is:

"That's interesting. Help me understand how you got there."

Not "that's low," not "thank you so much," not "that works." Just "interesting" — and a question that makes them justify the number, not you.
